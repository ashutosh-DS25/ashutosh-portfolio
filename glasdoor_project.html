Job Market Insights from Glassdoor Listings

This project uses data scraped from Glassdoor job listings to analyze salary patterns and predict compensation for data-related roles. The dataset includes job title, company details, rating, size, ownership type, location, and job descriptions. The goal was to identify what factors most influence salaries and build a reliable machine learning model for salary prediction.

The project begins with importing the data and exploring its structure. The Salary Estimate column was cleaned by removing text symbols, splitting ranges, and converting all salaries to annual figures. Hourly salaries were standardized using the formula (per_hour × 40 × 52). Missing or invalid entries like “-1” were removed. The Company Name column was cleaned to remove appended ratings, and country and city were extracted from the Location field for geographical analysis.

Key skills such as Python, Machine Learning, AWS, Power BI, and SQL were extracted from the job descriptions using string matching and regex. Additional features like company size, ownership type, and sector were grouped to reduce category complexity. Columns with too many missing or undefined values (like Revenue and Competitors) were dropped after analysis.

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) revealed that most jobs belonged to the IT, Biotech, and Business Services sectors, with New York, San Francisco, and Cambridge as the top hiring locations. Smaller companies (1–50 employees) and very large ones (10,000+) showed slightly higher median salaries compared to mid-sized firms. Skills like C++, Python, and Machine Learning were among the most in-demand.

For modeling, the data was split into train and test sets. A preprocessing pipeline combined Ordinal and OneHot encoders for categorical features while leaving binary skill columns unchanged. Multiple regression models were tested including Linear Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, XGBoost, and SVR.

Among all, XGBoost performed the best, achieving a Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of approximately $14,342. Feature importance analysis revealed that job title, company rating, sector, and technical skills (Python, SQL, AWS) had the highest impact on salary prediction.

This project demonstrates the entire data science lifecycle — from data wrangling and feature extraction to modeling and optimization. It not only predicts salaries effectively but also provides insights into skill demand and company hiring trends across industries.
