Turning Retail Data into Insights: Super Store Sales Dashboard

This project showcases an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze and visualize the performance of a Superstore’s sales data. The dataset contains more than 9,000 transactional entries across different product categories, regions, and time periods. The goal was to track key business metrics like total sales, profit, and delivery trends while identifying which areas, products, and customers contribute the most to growth.

The dashboard was built after cleaning and transforming the dataset in Power BI. Columns such as State, Region, Category, Sub-Category, Sales, Profit, Quantity, Payment Mode, and Ship Mode were structured and linked through relational modeling. Calculated measures were created using DAX for Total Sales (1.6M), Total Profit (0.18M), Total Quantity (22K), and Average Delivery Days (3.9).

Each visual in the dashboard answers a key business question. The Category-wise Sales vs Profit chart reveals that Technology leads with the highest sales (~0.6M), followed by Office Supplies (~0.5M) and Furniture (~0.4M). However, despite strong sales, Furniture shows thinner profit margins, signaling higher operational costs or discounts. The Sub-category analysis highlights that Phones, Chairs, and Binders are the top-selling products, while Tables and Bookcases often drag down profitability due to higher returns and shipment costs.

The Regional view provides a clear pattern — the West region contributes the largest share of profit, while the Central region underperforms, possibly due to low-volume orders or poor product mix. The Sales by State map further confirms that states like California, New York, and Texas dominate in both revenue and profit, with combined sales crossing 0.9M.

On the Customer segment front, the Consumer segment brings in the highest revenue (~0.8M), while Corporate and Home Office contribute smaller but steadier profits. The Payment Mode breakdown shows that Online payments account for over 55% of total sales, followed by Cash on Delivery (~30%) and Cards (~15%), showing a clear customer preference for digital transactions.

Temporal analysis through Yearly and Monthly trends reveals seasonal spikes in Q4 2019 and mid-2020, likely due to holiday sales or promotional campaigns. Despite the pandemic dip in early 2020, the business showed a strong recovery in later months, maintaining profitability through adaptive category pricing.

Overall, the dashboard converts complex retail data into a visually rich and decision-ready story. It allows users to filter by year or region, compare product performance, and evaluate delivery efficiency, making it a practical tool for tracking business health and operational strategy.
